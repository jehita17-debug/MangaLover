Two of the smartest students in an elite academy are in love and would rather die than admit it, because whoever confesses first "loses." That's the joke, and Akasaka spins it into elaborate psychological skirmishes over things as small as sharing an umbrella — narrated like a war documentary, escalating like a heist. For a long stretch it's simply one of the funniest manga around.

Read this when you want comedy with real architecture. The gags are structured like chess problems, the supporting cast steadily steals the show, and the series is smart enough to know that pride is a comedy until it isn't. Somewhere past the midpoint the armor starts to crack, and the laughs make room for something that genuinely moves.

If you need your romance to progress quickly, the early stalemate is the format; patience is part of the deal. But if you're in the mood for a love-as-warfare comedy that earns its eventual sincerity, Kaguya-sama is the gold standard — a series that makes two people being too stubborn to say "I like you" into something hilarious and, finally, touching.
