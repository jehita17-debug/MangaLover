A girl treated as a servant by her own family is married off to a military man rumored to be cruel — and braces for more of the only treatment she's ever known. The quiet devastation of the early chapters is how thoroughly she's been taught she deserves nothing, so that ordinary kindness reads to her as a trap. Watching her slowly dare to believe otherwise is the whole emotional engine, and it's a strong one.

Read this when you want a fairy tale that takes emotional damage seriously. The period setting carries a touch of the supernatural, but the heart is the romance: two guarded people, each braced for rejection, learning the unfamiliar grammar of being cared for. It's soft, earnest, and unafraid of sentiment.

If you want grit or moral complexity, this isn't that — it wears its heart openly and trusts you to want the happy ending. Lean in. My Happy Marriage is comfort romance done with real feeling, a Cinderella story whose magic isn't the supernatural touches but the slow, frightening miracle of being treated gently for the first time.
